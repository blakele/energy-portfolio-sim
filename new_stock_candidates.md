## TIER 1 CANDIDATES (Highest Conviction)

### 1. VRT -- Vertiv Holdings
**Sector:** Data Center Cooling / Power Infrastructure

**Thesis Fit:** The direct "picks and shovels" play for AI datacenter power and cooling. As AI clusters push 120-150kW per rack, traditional air cooling fails physically. Vertiv supplies uninterruptible power systems, power distribution units, thermal management, and liquid cooling. Mass adoption of liquid cooling is underway.

**SEC Filing Highlights:**
- **Revenue:** Q3 2025 net sales $2.68B (+29% YoY). FY2026 guidance: $13.25-13.75B (~28% organic growth)
- **Backlog:** Record **$15 billion** (book-to-bill ~2.9x). Provides visibility through 2027
- **Debt-to-equity:** 0.83x (dramatically improved from >6x five years ago)
- **Free cash flow:** FY2025 adjusted FCF $1.47-1.53B. ~25% annual FCF growth expected
- **Valuation:** FY2026 EPS guidance $5.97-6.07 (12% beat vs. consensus)
- **Insider activity:** CTO/EVP sold shares in late 2025 after massive run. Selling into strength, not distress

**Catalysts:** Liquid cooling adoption inflection; $15B backlog conversion; 252% organic order growth

**How it complements existing portfolio:** Fills the "datacenter infrastructure" gap. Your existing picks power the datacenters (CEG, VST) and build the grid to connect them (GEV, ETN). Vertiv keeps them cool and running. Direct revenue linkage to hyperscaler capex.

### 2. PWR -- Quanta Services
**Sector:** Grid Infrastructure / Electrical Construction

**Thesis Fit:** Largest specialty contractor for electric power infrastructure in North America. Every new datacenter campus needs transmission lines, substations, and grid interconnections built -- Quanta does that work. The NiSource deal alone covers ~3 GW of power generation and supporting grid infrastructure for datacenter customers.

**SEC Filing Highlights:**
- **Revenue:** FY2025 $28.48B (+20.3% YoY). 2026 guidance up to $33.75B
- **Backlog:** Record **$44.0 billion** (grew from $39.2B mid-year). Heavily weighted to Electric Infrastructure
- **Debt-to-equity:** 0.65-0.72x (manageable for capital-intensive contractor)
- **Free cash flow:** TTM levered FCF ~$708M
- **EPS:** Adjusted $10.75 in 2025; double-digit growth guided for 2026
- **Insider activity:** Not materially noteworthy

**Catalysts:** $44B backlog conversion; Cupertino Electric acquisition integration; NiSource 3 GW buildout; grid modernization regulatory tailwinds

**How it complements existing portfolio:** Your portfolio has equipment makers (GEV, ETN) but no one to install the equipment. Quanta is the labor/construction layer of the grid buildout thesis.

## TIER 2 CANDIDATES (Strong Conviction)

### 3. NRG -- NRG Energy
**Sector:** Merchant Power / Independent Power Producer

**Thesis Fit:** Most aggressive pure-play IPP targeting AI datacenter load. LS Power acquisition doubled generation to ~26 GW. 5.4 GW joint venture with GE Vernova and Kiewit to build gas plants specifically for datacenter demand in ERCOT and PJM. Vertically integrated (generation + retail).

**SEC Filing Highlights:**
- **Revenue:** FY2025 ~$29.8B (+5.9% YoY). Adjusted EBITDA $4.1B
- **Datacenter pipeline:** 295 MW in premium long-term datacenter retail agreements. 5.4 GW development with GE Vernova
- **Debt-to-equity:** 6.15x (elevated from LS Power acquisition -- KEY RISK)
- **Free cash flow:** $2.2B FCF before growth investments
- **EPS:** Adjusted $8.24 in 2025. 14%+ annual growth target through 2030
- **Insider activity:** Net selling of 172,556 shares ($27.6M) in past quarter. Insiders own 0.86%

**Catalysts:** LS Power integration; 5.4 GW GE Vernova gas plant development; first Texas datacenter power-on H2 2026; TEF-financed 1.5 GW coming online June 2026

**Risk note:** The 6.15x D/E is the discount. If they execute on deleveraging, significant upside. If not, it's a drag.

## TIER 3 CANDIDATES (Speculative)

### 10. LEU -- Centrus Energy
**Sector:** Uranium Enrichment / HALEU

**Thesis Fit:** Only U.S.-licensed uranium enrichment company. Only private HALEU (High-Assay Low-Enriched Uranium) producer in the Western world. HALEU is required fuel for next-gen SMRs (OKLO, NuScale, etc.). As nuclear renaissance accelerates, fuel supply is a critical bottleneck -- Centrus is the bottleneck-breaker.

**SEC Filing Highlights:**
- **Revenue:** FY2025 $448.7M. Q3 2025 +30% YoY. Technical Solutions +31%
- **Contract backlog:** ~$2.1B in contingent LEU sales commitments
- **Major contract:** $900M DOE HALEU task order (Jan 2026)
- **Debt-to-equity:** 3.34x (though cash tripled to $2.0B)
- **Free cash flow:** $31.3M FY2025 (investing heavily in centrifuge expansion)
- **2026 guidance:** Revenue $425-475M. Capex $350-500M for centrifuge buildout

**Catalysts:** $900M DOE HALEU task order; centrifuge manufacturing with Fluor; only private HALEU producer; nuclear renaissance policy tailwinds

**Risk:** Binary upside from HALEU monopoly but volatile revenue and massive capital deployment. Pairs with OKLO thesis but carries similar speculative profile.

- **Insider activity:** Not materially noteworthy

**Catalysts:** Meta $10B Louisiana campus; Google $4B Arkansas facility; 2.3 GW new gas plants; $43B capex through 2029

**Note:** Regulated utility = slower appreciation but very low downside. Steady compounder with direct hyperscaler contracts. Different risk profile than CEG/VST (regulated vs. merchant).
