## 1. MAJOR DIP EVENTS (2024-2026)

### Event A: DeepSeek Shock (January 27, 2025) -- THE BIGGEST SINGLE-DAY DIP

**What happened:** Chinese AI startup DeepSeek announced its R1 model could perform advanced AI tasks at a reported cost of just $5.6M in compute, vs. hundreds of millions for US models. This questioned the entire thesis that AI would drive massive electricity demand growth.

**Single-day declines:**
- VST (Vistra): -28% to -30% (worst in S&P 500 that day)
- CEG (Constellation Energy): -21%
- GEV (GE Vernova): -21.5%
- OKLO: -25%
- CCJ (Cameco): -15%
- Natural gas futures: -5.9%

**From Jan peaks to troughs (over following weeks):**
- VST: -35% from January highs
- CEG: -35% from January peak, fell below 200-day moving average
- GEV: -30%, erased nearly all post-Trump-election gains

**Why it was so severe:** AI data center power demand represents ~75% of projected US electricity demand growth through 2030-35. DeepSeek's efficiency claims directly attacked the core bull thesis for these stocks.

---

### Event B: FERC Rejection of Talen/Amazon Nuclear Colocation Deal (November 2024)

**What happened:** FERC rejected the behind-the-meter nuclear power deal between Amazon (AWS) and Talen Energy, where a data center would plug directly into a nuclear plant. This sent shockwaves through the entire nuclear-power-for-AI trade.

**Impact:**
- CEG: Dropped ~10% despite reporting strong Q3 earnings
- Broad nuclear/data-center stocks sold off in sympathy
- Raised regulatory risk premium across the sector

**Resolution:** In December 2025, FERC reversed course and approved a clear pathway for "colocation," allowing data centers to sit directly next to power plants -- a major win for CEG, VST, and others.

---

### Event C: Interest Rate / Inflation Fears (Throughout 2023-2024)

**What happened:** Sustained high interest rates crushed capital-intensive energy infrastructure stocks, especially renewables.

**Impact on specific stocks:**
- NEE (NextEra): Traded 25% below all-time highs in mid-2024. NextEra Partners' model became "stuck" because high rates made financing new projects via stock/debt issuance dilutive.
- BEP (Brookfield Renewable): Hit all-time low of $21.35 on April 16, 2024 (now ~$32). Traded 45% below ATH at one point.
- ETN (Eaton): Impacted by weaker eMobility/vehicle segment + Hurricane Helene disruptions + tariff fears. Down ~16% YTD in early 2025.

**Natural gas names (KMI, LNG, EQT) were MORE RESILIENT** to interest rate fears because their revenue is driven by commodity prices and contracts rather than project financing.

---

### Event D: Trump Election / Renewable Policy Fears (November 2024)

**What happened:** Trump's election raised fears about rollback of renewable energy incentives (IRA, tax credits).

**Impact:**
- Renewable stocks (NEE, BEP) sold off on fears of policy rollback
- Nuclear stocks (CEG, CCJ, OKLO) actually RALLIED initially because Trump was seen as pro-nuclear
- GEV benefited from both sides (gas turbines + nuclear servicing)

---

### Event E: Trump Tariff Escalation (February 2026 -- CURRENT)

**What happened:** On Feb 21, 2026, Trump raised global tariffs to 15% (from 10%) after the Supreme Court struck down IEEPA tariffs. This is happening RIGHT NOW.

**Impact (as of Feb 23-25, 2026):**
- Dow fell 823 points (-1.66%) on Feb 23
- VST: Down ~15% from recent levels
- CEG: Down ~11%
- GEV: Down ~10%

  2. Higher costs for steel, aluminum, copper used in energy infrastructure buildout

---

### Event F: EQT-Equitrans Merger Disruption (March-July 2024)

**What happened:** EQT announced and completed a $35B acquisition of Equitrans Midstream in July 2024, creating a vertically integrated natural gas business.

**Impact:** EQT stock saw volatility around the merger announcement and completion. The combined entity has a free cash flow breakeven at ~$2.00/MMBtu natural gas, with $425M+ in annual synergies.

---

### Event G: CEG-Calpine Mega-Merger (Jan 2025 announcement, Jan 2026 close)

**What happened:** CEG announced $26.6B acquisition of Calpine (Jan 2025), completed Jan 7, 2026. Created nation's largest power producer.

**Impact on CEG:** Stock initially SURGED ~58% in 2025 on deal announcement. However, the deal required DOJ settlement (Calpine had to sell 6 gas plants). The stock has since given back gains, trading ~$288 vs. $353 at start of 2026.

## 2. CORRELATION DURING SELLOFFS

### These stocks DID dip together during the DeepSeek selloff -- but with different magnitudes:

**Highest Correlation Group (AI-Power-Demand plays):**
- CEG, VST, GEV, OKLO -- ALL dropped 20-30% on DeepSeek Day
- These are the "picks and shovels" of AI energy and move almost in lockstep during AI-related selloffs
- They have effectively become high-beta tech proxies

**Moderate Correlation (Nuclear supply chain):**
- CCJ dropped 15% on DeepSeek Day -- correlated but less extreme
- CCJ is somewhat insulated because uranium demand isn't solely AI-driven (geopolitical, defense, global decarbonization)

**Lower Correlation (Natural gas / midstream):**
- KMI, LNG, EQT -- LESS correlated with AI selloffs
- Natural gas futures fell 5.9% on DeepSeek Day but these companies have diversified revenue streams (LNG exports, pipeline contracts, commodity trading)
- KMI is up 13.5% over the past month even as CEG/VST are down

**Different Drivers (Renewables / Electricals):**
- NEE, BEP -- More sensitive to INTEREST RATES than to AI narratives
- ETN -- Correlated with both industrial/manufacturing cycle AND AI data center buildout
- BEP hit its low in April 2024 (interest rate fears), while CEG/VST were surging

### Key Insight: Utilities sector has LOST its traditional defensive character
The utilities sector now closely tracks the S&P 500 due to AI exposure. In 2024-2025, utilities spent much more time correlated with broad market direction than in previous years (2018, 2022). This means:
- CEG/VST/GEV no longer hedge against market downturns
- They amplify market moves (higher beta)
- KMI/LNG/EQT still behave more like traditional energy/commodity stocks

**CCJ:** Recovered and trading near multi-year highs (~$113-121). Uranium fundamentals (supply deficit) provided floor.

### Interest Rate Recovery (BEP, NEE):

Commodity-linked names (KMI, LNG, CCJ) tend to recover faster because their fundamentals are less narrative-dependent.

## 5. TECHNICAL INDICATORS AND PATTERNS

### What Has Worked Historically for These Stocks:

#### RSI (Relative Strength Index) -- MOST USEFUL INDICATOR
- **CEG hit most oversold RSI levels during DeepSeek crash** since its last major rebound. RSI currently ~31-33 (near oversold threshold of 30). Historically, buying CEG when RSI drops below 30 has yielded strong bounces.
- VST and GEV similarly hit oversold RSI during DeepSeek, then recovered to new highs within months.
- **Pattern:** RSI below 30 on these high-momentum names has been a reliable buy signal when the underlying bull thesis (AI power demand) remains intact.

#### 200-Day Moving Average -- KEY SUPPORT/RESISTANCE
- **CEG fell below its 200-day MA** after DeepSeek -- this was a sell signal that preceded further weakness. Currently trading below 200-day MA again ($321 vs. price ~$288).
- **When these stocks are ABOVE their 200-day MA, trend is bullish.** Breaks below = caution warranted.
- VST has tended to find support at or near its 200-day MA during pullbacks.

#### Volume Confirmation
- High-volume selloffs (like DeepSeek Day) that are followed by declining volume on subsequent down days = sign of selling exhaustion.
- Rallies on increasing volume = confirmation of trend reversal.

#### Moving Average Crossovers
- 50-day MA crossing ABOVE 200-day MA ("golden cross") = bullish signal
- 50-day MA crossing BELOW 200-day MA ("death cross") = bearish signal
- These have been useful for KMI and LNG which trend more smoothly than the volatile nuclear names.

#### Fibonacci Retracement Levels
- After the DeepSeek crash, CEG bounced near the 61.8% Fibonacci retracement of its prior rally -- a classic support level.
- OKLO has been so volatile that Fibonacci levels are less reliable.

#### Sector-Specific Patterns
- **Earnings Gap-and-Fill:** CEG, VST, and GEV tend to gap up or down on earnings, then spend weeks filling the gap. Patience after earnings moves is rewarded.
- **Natural Gas Price Correlation:** EQT and KMI track Henry Hub natural gas prices closely. When nat gas is at/near $2.00/MMBtu (EQT's breakeven), EQT becomes a leveraged bet on gas price recovery.
- **Uranium Spot Price:** CCJ correlates with uranium spot price but trades at a premium due to long-term contracts. Watch uranium spot for leading signals.
